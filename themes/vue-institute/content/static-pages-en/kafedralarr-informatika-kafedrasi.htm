The department was founded in January 2019 and today the department of "Informatics" is part of the faculty of "Exact Sciences" and is graduating in the following direction:

Baccalaureate - “Pedagogical Education” (training profile: methods of teaching informatics).

Today, a unique multi-disciplinary team has formed at the department, in which specialists, scientists and practitioners of various scientific fields work, such as information technology, computer linguistics, information security, programming, computer graphics, the use of information technologies in national and agricultural sectors and others.

Leaders and leading specialists of organizations and enterprises whose activities are related to the direction of the main professional educational programs being implemented are involved in the training sessions. These are specialists in the field of teacher education, information security, web design and computer graphics, as well as programming.

 

To intensify the educational process, the teaching staff of the department uses modern teaching methods, uses multimedia tools, excursions to leading higher educational institutions in this area and information and communication technologies for conducting classes.

Of great importance for consolidating theoretical material and mastering the skills of a teacher are the practices conducted in secondary schools.

Creative contacts connect the department with the leading universities and research institutes of the Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Uzbekistan: scientific research institute of irrigation and water problems, National University of Uzbekistan named after Mirzo Ulugbek, Tashkent State Pedagogical University named after Nizami, Tashkent University of Information Technologies, Tashkent Institute of Irrigation and Agricultural Mechanization Engineers and secondary schools.

The department carries out international cooperation in the implementation of joint projects, scientific internships and the organization of educational practices with several European universities:

  •  Chimkent District Innovation University (Kazakhstan);
  •  South Kazakhstan State University named after Mukhtar Avezov (Kazakhstan);
  •  Novosibirsk State Pedagogical University (Russia);
  •  European University of Lefke (Northern Cyprus).

 

The department conducts research in the following priority areas:

 

  • the innovation cluster method for teaching computer science and information technology in secondary schools;
  • Actual problems of using information technologies in the training system;
  • improvement of teaching methods when designing training sessions;
  • development and implementation of electronic resources for teaching computer science in secondary schools;
  • a mathematical model of land reclamation and water conservation problems using interpolymer complexes.

Teachers of the department regularly participate in interregional and international seminars and conferences.
